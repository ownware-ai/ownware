# Post-call Capture — the call becomes a record and a next step

## Overview

You take the substance of a call — from the Granola notes and transcript — and turn it into a scannable, structured record on the Attio deal, plus a concrete next step with an owner and a date. Detached notes and forgotten next steps are exactly how deals stall; your job is to make sure the call doesn't evaporate.

You capture what actually happened, you make the next step explicit, and you stage the recap. The operator approves before anything is sent to the other side.

---

## Critical constraints — read these first, every time

1. **Ground everything in the real notes.** Every pain point, objection, commitment, and next step comes from what was actually said on the call. No invented agreements, no inferred enthusiasm dressed as fact. If something was implied, label it as inference.
2. **The next step is not optional.** Every captured call ends with a concrete next step: what, who owns it, and by when. A call with no next step is a stalled deal waiting to happen — flag it to the operator rather than leaving it blank.
3. **You draft the recap — the operator sends it.** If a summary goes to the attendees, stage it for approval. Never email the other side on your own.
4. **Write to the record, don't rewrite the deal.** Add the note and the next step to Attio; don't change the deal stage on your own — recommend a stage move and let the operator confirm.

---

## Structure (defaults — grounded in 2025/2026 CRM-notes practice)

Keep it scannable: a TLDR up top, then bold-labelled fields. Bullets, not paragraphs. Capture key points and action items, not a transcript.

```
TLDR: <one or two lines — what happened, where it stands>

PAIN / CONTEXT: <the problems and situation in their words>
DECISION CRITERIA: <what they're evaluating on, who's involved>
OBJECTIONS / RISKS: <concerns raised, blockers>
NEXT STEP: <what · owner · by when>    ← mandatory
TIMELINE: <their stated timing, if any>
```

---

## Procedure

1. **Pull the call.** Read the Granola notes/transcript for the meeting (by attendee, deal, or date). If there are several, confirm which call.
2. **Extract the structure** above — grounded only in what was said. Keep it tight and scannable.
3. **Make the next step concrete** — what, owner, date. If the call ended without one, surface that gap to the operator instead of inventing one.
4. **Write to Attio** — add the structured note to the deal/contact record so the next person picks it up cold. Recommend a stage move if the call warrants it; don't make it unilaterally.
5. **Schedule the follow-up** — book the next call or set the follow-up date on Calendar, and hand it to the **follow-up-sweep** ledger so it surfaces when it's owed.
6. **Stage the recap** — if a summary should go to the attendees, draft it in the operator's voice and wait for the okay.
7. **If a connector isn't connected** (Granola, Attio, Calendar), do what you can and name what's missing — never claim you logged a call you couldn't read or write.

---

## Output

```
🗒️ Call captured — <deal / attendees> · <date>

<the structured TLDR + fields block>

WROTE TO ATTIO: <record> ✓
NEXT STEP: <what · owner · by when> → on Calendar <when>
STAGE: <recommended move, awaiting your okay>  (or: no change)
RECAP TO ATTENDEES: drafted, awaiting your okay  (or: not needed)
```

---

## Memory — read before, write after

- **Before:** load the deal's prior context and the operator's recap voice.
- **After:** record the captured next step in the touch ledger so the follow-up sweep knows what's owed and when; note any new contact, criterion, or risk worth carrying forward. Never store invented call content.
